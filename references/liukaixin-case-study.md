# Liu Kaixin Case Study

This case came from a 3h44m vertical livestream in which Tuobuhua recommended Liu Kaixin's two books: `我们可以坦然生长` and `愿你可以自在张扬`.

## What Happened

The first rough version was too abrupt and then too long. Through review, it became a 10m54s version with:

- a strong opening line about life force
- restored context about who Liu Kaixin is
- complete background rather than isolated product selling
- wheel-chair B-roll as picture-in-picture
- Liu Kaixin photo and book images at the first relevant moment
- subtitle cleanup using house style
- emotional section preserved instead of covered by full-screen text
- restrained closing reason to read the books

## Lessons

ASR first:

- The book was visible in the livestream for a long time, so visual scanning could not locate the real story window.

Opening:

- A strong hook works only if the next minute repairs context.

Subtitle:

- Raw ASR left mistakes such as `何菜头` instead of `和菜头`.
- Names, places, gender pronouns, and book titles must be checked against a glossary.

Emotion:

- Crying is not just a conclusion. The preceding quote and buildup are part of the meaning.

Cover:

- The user preferred a bright person-first cover, two-line centered title, bold yellow-orange text, black stroke, light mask, and no lower-left small text.

Versioning:

- Keeping every cover and render version made it safe to compare and revert.

## Reusable Rule

For book-recommendation livestreams:

1. Start with a vivid judgment or scene.
2. Establish the person before selling the book.
3. Explain why this is not ordinary inspirational content.
4. Use assets as supporting PIP, not as a replacement for the speaker.
5. Preserve one original-text passage if it explains the emotional reason to read.
6. Close with viewer value rather than hard sales pressure.
