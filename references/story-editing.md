# Story Editing

## Principle

A short video is not a shorter transcript. It is a self-contained story for a cold viewer.

## Good 5-10 Minute Structure

1. Hook: strong claim, surprising image, conflict, or question.
2. Context repair: who the person is and why the topic matters.
3. Stakes: what makes the situation difficult or meaningful.
4. Turn: a scene or line that changes how the viewer understands the topic.
5. Evidence: original quote, product/book detail, lived example, or outside voice.
6. Emotional peak: preserve buildup and pauses.
7. Close: practical reason to read/watch/buy/act, without sounding like a hard sell.

## Segment Selection Rules

Keep a segment when:

- it can be understood without watching the full livestream
- it advances the central story
- it contains a clear claim, scene, emotional beat, or evidence
- it does not depend on an omitted setup

Drop or trim a segment when:

- it repeats a previous idea without adding force
- it names a metaphor before explaining it
- it changes topic without clean transition
- it adds selling pressure but not viewer value

## Opening Rules

The opening 10 seconds can begin with the strongest line, but the next minute must answer:

- Who is this about?
- What is happening?
- Why should I trust or care?
- What is the promise of the video?

## Emotional Rules

- Do not cut directly from a setup into a tearful conclusion.
- Keep the sentence or quote that causes the emotion.
- Do not cover eyes, mouth, or tears with full-screen text.
- Let silence or a breath remain if it carries meaning.

## Product/Book Recommendation Rules

For book/product content, avoid pure sales posture. The sequence should be:

1. Why this person/story matters.
2. What the book/product actually is.
3. What idea or experience it gives the viewer.
4. Who it is suitable for.
5. Only then: why to buy/read/try.
